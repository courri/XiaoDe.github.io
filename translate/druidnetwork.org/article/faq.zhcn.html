德鲁伊做什么？

　　这是一个很简单的问题，经常被问起，但着实难以回答。德鲁伊用神圣的态度对待自然、祖先和传统，而德鲁伊的实践也需要进行大量专心致志的学习。

　　学习自然环境：德鲁伊会庆祝四季的更替循环，关注人类对于生态环境的影响。他们会积极参与各种环保行动，身体力行来保护地球及其孕育的人类以外的生命。

　　学习人的本性：德鲁伊努力提升自我认知，寻求对情绪、理智、意识和自由意志的理解。他们会寻找灵感的源泉，培养自己的创新意识，并努力发挥这种意识给民族和社会带来激励。

　　学习本民族的历史、文化和风土人情：德鲁伊会学习本民族的传统、历史乃至史前传说，并乐于在古老的神话和民间传说中探索祖先的故事。许多人还会学习本民族的语言（比如凯尔特语）、音乐、戏曲、传统技艺及传承下来的民俗表演等。

　　在这样的学习实践中，德鲁伊将发现自己的神，并通过祭品、祈祷、创造以及恪守活出荣耀之承诺，展现自己对其的虔诚。

　　换句话说，德鲁伊的生活中没有哪个部分是不会接触到神、祖先和自然环境的——生炉子、打理花园、照顾孩子、看日出……而德鲁伊的旅程，即是学习如何以尊重、负责的态度去和万物互动——这便是一种虔诚的行为。

“德鲁伊”一词何意？

　　（译者：“德鲁伊”，也写作“德鲁依”、“德鲁伊特”或“督伊德”，是由英语单词“druid”音译过来的。“德鲁伊”这种译法受日语译法影响，与英语发音差异较大。但巧合的是在爱尔兰语中“druid”是“drui”的复数，而单数形式“drui”的发音与“德鲁伊”较相似。“督伊德”更接近英语发音，但是没有前者流行。）

　　关于“druid”一词的来源，说法有很多种。词源学中一种较为公认的说法是，该词来源于两个古英语单词——前半部分“dru”是“橡树”或者“门口”的意思，而后半部分则来自“wisdom”（智慧）的缩略形式“wid”。

　　（译者：也有人认为，“druid”一词由来自不同语言的两部分构成：前半部很大程度上和希腊文的“drus”相关，是橡树的意思；而后半部与印欧语系的词尾“-wid”相似，意为“去了解”。所以，“德鲁伊”直译为“橡木贤者”，即“了解橡树的人”。注：希腊语同古德鲁伊们的语言凯尔特语一样属于印欧语系。）

　　总的来说，这个词意味着某人熟谙自然的智慧，或者具有这样一种内在品质。

德鲁伊是宗教吗？

　　如果我们把宗教定义为一种精神的实践，包括对神或神圣事物的虔诚、敬畏和崇拜，那么德鲁伊确实是宗教。

　　然而，因为很多德鲁伊发现，“宗教”这个词已经和“森严的阶级”、“教条主义”、“基于礼拜式的一神论”等概念纠缠不清了，所以有些时候我们会回避这个词本身。通常，首选的词是“灵性”，比起说成“德鲁伊教”，把德鲁伊描述成一条“灵性的道路”给人的感觉更加自主和自由。但尽管如此，德鲁伊依然是宗教。

　　德鲁伊网（The Druid Network）在2010年成功得到（英国）慈善事务委员会的认可，被公认为是一个发展德鲁伊宗教的团体。虽然德鲁伊网融合了各种信仰或无信仰的人，但是它的章程中指出，其原则和目标，是以把德鲁伊视为一种宗教为基础的，无论是古代还是现代德鲁伊。

所有德鲁伊都是多神信仰吗？

　　并非如此。德鲁伊没有显示的教条去严格定义怎样理解、感知神与神性，取而代之的是允许每个人自行探索、体验他们自己与神性的连接。事实上，这种多元主义是德鲁伊思想的核心——在德鲁伊的实践中，体验神性远比接受任何强加的信仰重要的多。

　　不过大多数德鲁伊都称自己是多神信仰，他们的神表达为存在的每一个方面——有的是显而易见的自然事物，比如日月、星辰、大地、河流、雨水、谷物、大山，有的是维持事物存在的自然力量，比如丰收、爱、重力、死亡、饥饿、命运、重生；还有的可能是祖先的智慧；是指引我们平衡人与人、人与自然关系的“道”；或者是公正、勇敢、仁爱、友善等品质的化身……有的德鲁伊从我们的神话、历史或民间故事中找到了其神的名字和教导；还有的则是用私人的或者非公开的名字称呼他们的神。

　　有一个重点要记住，那就是即使一个德鲁伊不积极信仰某个特定的神，他也不会否认该神的存在。多神信仰的德鲁伊可以选择只培养自己与某些神的关系，而对其他的神不关注。例如，某个德鲁伊可能只关注来自当地的神，比如来自流过村子的小河，来自大海（如果他住在海边的话），来自一座当地的山，来自土地本身（如果他以种地为生的话），或者来自这片大地上曾经被祖先们崇拜过的事物（比如在当地发现的英国罗马文化铭文）……很多德鲁伊会与两三个神保持较强的关系，与十一二个或更多神保持良好的关系，并且可能对二三十个其他的神有所了解。并且，德鲁伊会接受所有神的存在，即使他对他们一无所知。

　　大多数德鲁伊也称自己为泛神信仰。对德鲁伊来说，“自然”一词被用来定义所有的存在：它包括任何事物，无论是人类还是非人类，无论在地球之上或者在亿万光年之外。由于德鲁伊信仰的多元化，他们往往不使用“宇宙”这个词，因为“宇宙”一词通常仅指我们所在的时空，或者暗示着未被证明的宇宙唯一的观点。而“自然”一词则是一个整体的概念，它包括我们的宇宙和所有可能存在的其他宇宙，包括所有的可能性。对泛神信仰者来说，这个“整体”也可以成为他们的神。

　　德鲁伊的泛神信仰有两种：有的把自然这个“整体”当成至高的存在、最伟大的神、世界的本源或终极奥义，以此为基础实践自己的泛神信仰。而另一些则不同，他们更愿意与许多具体的神建立联系，通过量的累积来达到“整体”的程度——也许把这种方式解释为多神信仰更合适。

所有德鲁伊都相信万物有灵吗？

　　德鲁伊信仰的本质便是万物有灵的思想传统，因为德鲁伊认为自然的方方面面都有其灵性或者是有灵魂的。也可以这么说，自然的每一层次、每一部分都有其固有的价值和目标。有的人会更进一步说，从微小的亚原子粒子到浩瀚的银河系，自然的每一个层面都是有意识的。

　　于是，德鲁伊们倾向于承认一棵树、一条河，或者一块石头具有灵性、灵魂，或者意识。这种意识不同于人类的意识，因为石头与人类的性质不同。然而，当德鲁伊用这种方式去感知石头时，他便可以认识到它的历史、它在自然中的地位，以及它那不可侵犯的尊严。通过这种方式，万物有灵的思想为我们尊重自然的方方面面提供了道德基础。

　　德鲁伊对万物有灵的理解也可以证明德鲁伊的思想不是二元论（二元论认为世界的本原是意识和物质两个实体）。德鲁伊否认任何超自然的存在：没有创世的“造物主”。相反，自然本身才是创造的力量，它不断地自我创造，没有起点和终点。（我们的宇宙或许有自己的起点和终点，但是我们所在的宇宙并不是自然的全部。译者注。）德鲁伊确信这种创造力体现在自然的方方面面中——宇宙的演变、生命的进化、物质的转化、能量的转移……这种创造、改变、发展的力量存在于每一个事物身上。

德鲁伊有自己的节日和纪念活动吗？

　　德鲁伊敬畏自然和季节更替，他们的庆祝活动也反映了这一点。无论是个人还是团体的庆祝活动，都围绕着这不停变化的“年的循环”而展开。从二十世纪中期开始，这个像车轮般滚动的“年的循环”被分成了八个节日：萨温节（10月31日）、冬至（12月21日）、依博克节（2月2日）、春分（3月20日）、五朔节（5月1日）、夏至（6月21日）、收获节（8月1日）和秋分（9月22日）。最被公众所熟知的节日也许是夏至，以及德鲁伊们在英国的巨石阵前举行的夏至庆祝活动。并不是所有德鲁伊都庆祝全部这些节日，有的只庆祝季节性的节日（萨温、依博克、五朔、收获），也有的只关注和太阳有关的节日（两分两至）。此外，还有些德鲁伊团体会庆祝月相的变化。

夏至那天所有德鲁伊都去巨石阵吗？

德鲁伊们在位于英国境内的巨石阵前举行和太阳有关的庆祝活动已经有很长的历史了，许多人都觉得这是一个非常重要的“圣地”。不过，因为德鲁伊们觉得自己的部族和家乡更重要，所以那些居住地离巨石阵很远，和巨石阵没什么联系的人往往更愿意在离自己家近的地方举行庆祝活动（不管是古代还是现代都一样）。 在巨石阵前举行的所有庆祝活动中，夏至日那天的应该是最著名的。不过，虽然现在有大量的人参加这个每年6月21日清晨举行的古老仪式，但德鲁伊们却很少在这一天去（在两万多名狂欢者中，德鲁伊通常不到几十个）。大部分德鲁伊团体都会在夏至日附近找个时间秘密地访问他们的“圣地”，避开人群，在安静平和的氛围中冥想，与夏至日灿烂的阳光，与身边这些奇迹般的石头，与创造了这些奇迹的伟大祖先们，一起冥想。giving them the peace and space to meditate with the stones, the ancestors and the sun. For more information on Stonehenge, see our Sacred Sites pages in What is Druidry.

历史上哪些人是德鲁伊？

Strictly speaking, when we ask 'who were the Druids', we are speaking of the intellectual caste of 'Celtic' society, what many understand to be preRoman culture of Iron Age Europe, through the first century BCE (before the Common Era or the birth of Christ). Druids were to the Celts what Brahmins were to within Hindu society: the leaders, lawyers, poets, teachers, physicians, artists, and priests of society. In this way, 'Druid' is a functional term, not an ethnic or religious one, albeit within its own cultural context. Many Druids are not so specific when they look back on the history of their heritage, acknowledging that the priests of that Iron Age society would have held the legacy of their ancestors. Extend Druidry into prehistory, many feel their Druidry roots back simply into the priesthood of ancient Britain and Europe.

现代怎么还会有德鲁伊？

No one can trace a birth lineage of Druids from old times to the present, though some try and some maintain documented claims to prove it. The spirit of the Green World, however, has a life of its own. It surrounds and enfolds us, whether human culture and history are aware of it or not. From time to time this spirit - the spirit of Druidry - breaks in upon the world anew, and expresses itself in new places and new contexts. In this way, contemporary Druids are both new, and old.

女性可以成为德鲁伊吗？在我的印象中，古代的德鲁伊都是一些白胡子老头！

Most of the artwork and poetry we have portraying Druids dates from the Romantic revival of the late 18th and early 19th centuries, a time of rampant patriarchy, and therefore women are noticeably absent. But in the old tales, Celtic women shared status and authority with men. To mention just three who were sorceresses as well as warriors - Scathach in Scotland, who trained the great Irish hero Cuchulain; Boudicca, Queen of the Iceni peoples in south eastern Britain; and Aoife in Ireland, who changed King Lir’s children into swans. Contemporary Druidry is practised by women and men alike, carrying on its established tradition. The balance is around equal, though on many internet forums it may feel as if there are more men than women posting comments, and in workshops there are often more more women than men present. At public ritual, there is often a more balanced ratio.

德鲁伊对其他宗教的态度是什么？

Druids are polytheistic and/or pantheistic, so their attitude is naturally pluralistic, and thus accepting of others' beliefs and ideas. A person's measure is taken by their actions, their generosity, honesty and responsibility, not by the names of the gods they swear by. For more information on interfaith, see our Interfaith pages in Druidry in Practice.

德鲁伊可以同时信仰基督教吗？

Holding a pluralistic perspective, it is possible that a Druid might find vallue in and respect the teachings of the Christian religion, and its heritage within Britain, including that of the Culdee or Celtic Christian Church.  However, the Druid does not acknowledge deity to be existent outside of Nature, for nothing is beyond Nature: the Druidic understanding is of Nature as All, in a process of perpetual self-creating.  This is not Christianity. At the same time, it is possible for a Christian to respect the teachings of Druidry, and to perceive the whole of Nature as created by his one god, and thus profoundly sacred.  However, the Christian god is supernatural, ie. transcendant of Nature.  This is not Druidry.

巫术崇拜和德鲁伊信仰的区别是什么？

This is a complex question that many have spent a good deal of time on, for answers can vary according to the individual practice of those who are responding to the question. If someone has studied a great deal of Wicca before moving into Druidry, their Druidic practice may well reflect that. The distinguishing features given here may appear generalisations and extremes, but they still hold some validity. In Wicca, it is common to honour the goddess and her divine consort, while Druids tend to be more polyethistic and animistic. Deity is often acknowledged as most important in Wicca, together with anthropomorphised spirits or entities, while Druidry tends to honour the environment, spirits of place, and the ancestors first. Druidry is very much focused upon Britain and Ireland (with some finding its heritage within other European landscapes), its gods and mythologies being of these islands, while Wicca more readily works with gods from any tradition, land and culture. Wicca often has a focus on creating change through spell working to improve lives, while the focus in Druidry is inspiration and creativity. In Wicca, the cardinal directions and four elements of earth, air, fire and water, are always key, while in Druidry these can be left out, the focus as often being upon the three worlds of earth, sea and sky. Wiccan ritual groups (covens) tend to be closed, held in private, and are often fairly secretive; Druid ritual groups (groves) are usually open. Wicca is generally an initiatory tradition and formally taught whereas Druidry is more personal and experiential, its teachings openly accessible. Wiccan ritual is more often held indoors, while Druid ritual is usually held outside.

我需要学习巴德、奥瓦德、德鲁伊的课程，或者其中的一部分吗？

Many teachers and teaching groups will give instruction in Druidry moving from one element of the tradition to the next, starting with Bard, moving through Ovate to Druid. Some Welsh and Irish systems include other elements of the tradition. This is thought to be traditional and useful, the student building upon skills one by one. However, many who have trained long and hard within Druidry, having studied all the levels, find they naturally tend towards the one where they are most skilled. Some don't proceed in training, stopping where they feel comfortable. Those who don't go through any formal training are as likely to feel they have skills naturally in one area or another, feeling the distinction between the three (or more) roles is not important.

我需要学习古代凯尔特语言吗？

Some Druid Orders, Groves or groups do insist that a student learns one of the old Celtic languages - Welsh, Breton, Manx, Irish Gaelic, Cornish or one of the Scottish languages. This is most often true of groups that are either based in the Celtic diaspora, especially Australia and North America, where Druidry is very much a religious tradition of ancestral roots, or of reconstructionist groups in Britain and Ireland. There is certainly often felt a rich benefit can be gained, and a deeper connection to the ancestors made, when we know the language. There are as many groups who feel it is more important to live honourably and pour time and energy into an immediately relevant skill or task than spend time learning an ancestral language if it is not local to where you are living.

有德鲁伊的神话传说吗？

The ancient Druids left no written texts of their own of any kind, so our perceptions of their beliefs and mythology have to be made by inference from two sources. The first are the accounts of Greek and Roman writers, who were their contemporaries, albeit often hostile and variably-informed ones. The second is the much later literatures of medieval Wales and Ireland. Both these types of source are extremely problematic and difficult to interpret. One topic that is not addressed in either type of source is the issue of creation myths. We have absolutely no idea whatsoever how the ancient druids or Celtic peoples viewed the creation of the Universe, or what deities, spirits, or cosmic processes they may have regarded as responisble for it. This is a disappointing answer, I know, but an honest one.

“我的祖父是德鲁伊，你们有他的会员记录吗？”

This is a question that we get asked quite often. The problem is that Druidry is not a unitary body, and there are various organisations that the said grandfather could have been a member of. However, having said that, there are two allied organisations that seem to have been responsible for a good deal of the said grandfathers, and those two organisations now still exist as one body, although on a much smaller scale than was once the case. In England is the Ancient Order of Druids. This was originally set up as a Friendly Society open only to males and operating under pseudo-masonic lines, even so far as the local groups being known as Lodges. Overseas the equivalent Order was the United Ancient Order of Druids and their groups were usually called Groves and they did allow female members their own groups known as Circles. Both these groupings now come under the International Grand Lodge of Druidism and any information that is still available may be sought through them via their website. The Ancient Order of Druids in the UK has its own website here. However not all of the old local groups returned their records to the national Grand Lodges when they dissolved but it may be possible to obtain information from local record offices in the relevant areas. A list of local repositories can be found here.

德鲁伊信仰是否涉及民族主义和种族主义？

To many Druids, a fundamental expression of their Druidic practice and perspective is a deep love of the land. Many experience a sense of devotion to the landscape where they live, and/or those that were the homelands of their ancestors. That notion of home reveals an emotional and spiritual connection, inspiring the individual to nurture, protect and care for the land. For some, this is may be no more than a few square miles; more often it is concentrated within twenty or thirty mile radius, but extends beyond that even further. For a good number, this sense of home embraces a huge chunk of land, or the entire island where they live - or their significant ancestors lived. When a Druid speaks of this devotion, it is an expression of their connection with the land. Woven into that is the history of the land together with heritage and local culture; in other words what is also important is the way in which the ancestors lived, loved and died, how they learned to engage with the land and each other, where they succeeded and where they failed. For someone intent on doing so, and if the boundaries of 'home' happen to co-incide with some current or former political boundary, it could be imagined that the Druid's devotion is a kind of nationalism. It may also be thought that a Druid's reverence for their ancestors (or sense of their presence, and desire to learn from them) is a rejection of other nationalities and cultures, being somehow quietly underpinned by xenophobia. Neither of these are valid understandings of Druidry. A Druid's love of home, expressed as devotion to the landscape and ancestors, can more accurately be equated to the love of another person; if we love one person, it does not imply that we think little of others, even less despise them. Druidry teaches us to accept and celebrate the diversity of the wholeness of nature.
